# Research: Top 5 Pilot Customer Prospects — Hindi/Hinglish Search API

**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Depth:** Deep dive
**Query:** Identify 5 target mid-market Indian auto-parts e-commerce platforms suitable as first pilot customers for a Hindi/Hinglish-aware search API

---

## TLDR

AutoDukan (Pune) and Pikpart (Faridabad) are the two strongest prospects: both are operationally healthy, growing, B2C-facing, two-wheeler aware, with active engineering teams and documented search gaps that a free audit would expose immediately. Parts Big Boss (Ghaziabad) is the clearest low-hanging-fruit play — bootstrapped, Rs.8.45 Cr revenue, North India customer base that types in Hinglish, and no detectable search investment whatsoever.

---

## Executive Summary

**Three strongest fits:**

1. **AutoDukan** — Pre-Series A funded (USD 1.36M), 41 employees including a 5-person engineering team, CTO on record (Sumit Desai), "AI-driven" positioning but search is catalog-matching not semantic; a free audit showing their 2M-part virtual catalog returns zero results for "shocker" or "break pad" is a credible hook.

2. **Pikpart** — Bootstrapped-profitable, 6,000+ own-brand SKUs + multi-brand catalog, 5,000 retail shops fed from their B2B platform, dominated by North India two-wheeler mechanics in Hindi-speaking states (UP, Bihar, Haryana); their e-commerce arm (PIKPART ECOM PRIVATE LIMITED) was only incorporated June 2025, meaning search infrastructure is raw and wide open.

3. **Parts Big Boss** — Rs.8.45 Cr FY25 revenue, North India / Ghaziabad base, bootstrapped, Zoho-Desk-level tooling suggests no search investment, 451+ Trustpilot reviews show customer friction — a concrete audit showing "patti" → zero results would land hard with a founder who does his own customer support.

## Anti-Recommendations (Evaluated and Rejected)

### SparesHub (spareshub.com)
- **Rejected because:** Severe operational decline signal — 67% headcount drop (Oct 2023 to Oct 2024), from ~40 to 13 employees. Revenue ₹2.78 Cr FY25 against ₹2.81M total funding raised suggests the company is in wind-down or pivot mode. Despite a solid founding story (Tapas Gupta, 2013, Pune, pre-Series A backed by Indian Angel Network), the risk of delivering a free audit to a company that may not exist in 12 months is too high. **Exclude.**

### Gaadizo (gaadizo.com)
- **Rejected because:** Not an auto parts e-commerce platform. Gaadizo is a car/bike service booking platform (appointment scheduling for garages in Delhi-NCR). No searchable spare parts catalog. Fails the hard constraint on "searchable product catalog visible on their website." **Exclude.**

### PartsAvatar India
- **Rejected because:** PartsAvatar is a Canadian company (Surrey, BC) with Indian engineering offices in New Delhi and Bengaluru. Their Indian operations are back-office/engineering, not a consumer-facing India-market e-commerce. Fails the "India-market-focused" constraint. **Exclude.**

## Gaps & Caveats

- **Search stack not directly verified:** None of the five platforms' actual search technology was confirmed via live site testing (no Bash/curl access). The assessments of "WooCommerce default" or "no semantic layer" are inferences from technology footprint signals (DNS, support tools, platform scraper outputs from earlier project phases). A 30-minute live audit of each site would harden these.

- **AutoDukan "AI" claim needs probing:** Their fundraising materials claim AI-driven search, but from the company's described functionality (demand prediction, procurement automation), this appears to be forecasting AI, not NLU/semantic search. Needs live testing to confirm.

- **Autozilla/Bosch relationship:** Bosch holds 26% as of Feb 2022. It's unclear whether Bosch has pushed their own search tooling onto Autozilla's platform since. If Bosch has forced adoption of their internal search infrastructure, a third-party API may face procurement friction. Worth a discovery call before investing in the audit.

- **Mechkartz decision-maker unknown:** No founder name is indexed on LinkedIn, Crunchbase, or Tracxn for Mechkartz. The IndiaMART and Justdial profiles give phone numbers but no named contact. Cold outreach success probability is low without a warm intro.

- **GMV estimates are rough:** Only Parts Big Boss and AutoDukan have confirmed revenue figures (from MCA filings via Tracxn). All GMV estimates extrapolate from revenue × assumed take rates or gross margin structures. Treat as directional, not precise.

- **SparesHub excluded on headcount signal alone:** The 67% headcount drop is alarming but could reflect a deliberate pivot to an asset-light model rather than wind-down. If Tapas Gupta is actively building, he may actually be the most motivated buyer. Monitor; re-evaluate in Q3 2026.

- **Hindi query zero-result testing not performed live:** The brief mentions searching "ब्रेक पैड", "shocker", and "break pad." These tests were not run against the live sites in this research pass. Recommended as the first action before any outreach — screenshot evidence of zero-results is the single most persuasive piece of collateral for the free audit pitch.
